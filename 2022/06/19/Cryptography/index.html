Classical Cipher

Basic Knowledge

A cryptosystem consists of five parts

  • plaintext space m - collection of plaintext to be encrypted
  • ciphertext space C - set of encrypted ciphertexts
  • key space K - collection of all keys
  • encryption transformation (algorithm) e: encryption controlled by encryption key
    Set of transformations, c = E (k, m) or c = EK (m)
  • decryption transformation (algorithm) d: decryption controlled by decryption key
    Set of transformations m = D (k, c) or m = D k (c)

A good cryptosystem should meet at least two conditions:

  • when the plaintext m and encryption key K1 are known, it is easy to calculate C =ek1 (m); When the ciphertext C and the decryption key K2 are known, it is easy to calculate M = D K2 (c);
  • when the decryption key K2 is unknown, it is impossible to recover from ciphertext C
    Output plaintext M.

Cryptanalyzers can usually attack under the following circumstances

  1. Ciphertext Only Attack
  2. Known Plaintext Attack
  3. Chosen Plaintext Attack
  4. Chosen Ciphertext Attack
    Ciphertext only attack has the weakest strength, and the attack strength increases accordingly.

Caesar Cipher

​ In 50 BC, the famous Caesar the great invented a ciphercalled the Caesar cipher. In Caesar’s cipher, each letter corresponds to the third letter after it, and then it is replaced. For example, “a” corresponds to “d”, “B” corresponds to “e”, and so on. If you reach the end of the alphabet, you go back to the beginning. For example, “Z” corresponds to “C”, “Y” corresponds to “B”, and “X” corresponds to “a”, thus forming a cycle. At that time, the Roman army used Caesar cipher to communicate.

Example: the plaintext is “Veni, vidi, vici”, ciphertext “yhql, ylgl, ylfl”.

Permutation Cipher

​ In the permutation cipher system, the words or letters in the plaintext are rearranged, the words or letters themselves remain unchanged, but the position has changed, forming the ciphertext, also known as the translocation cipher.
​ The simplest translocation cipher adopts the message inversion method, that is, the message is inverted in sequence according to the word order, and cut into a fixed length letter group to form a ciphertext.

Substitution Cipher

​ The encryption of substitution cipher is to replace each letter of plaintext with another letter in the alphabet. The substitution first replaces the plaintext letter with an equivalent decimal number, and then performs operations with the replaced decimal number.

​ According to the replacement of each letter one by one or multiple letters at the same time, classical cipher are divided into single table replacement cipher and multi table replacement cipher.

​ The single table substitution cipher can be divided into

  • add cipher
  • multiplication cipher
  • affine cipher
  • key phrase in place of cipher
Single table substitution cipher affine cipher

Ciphertext: y
